Project #3: Experimental Clock

Description

For Project 3, we were told to think about different ways of tracking time, and create a "clock" that visualizes our chosen method. It just could not show the traditional hours, minutes, and seconds. I created a due date clock, as the inputted due date gets closer, the pulse of the heart increases and the background color changes based on how much time is left.

Design Process

We first started out with ideas of time. Some ideas of tracking time I had were tracking the amount of music we listen to, or the amount of steps taken/miles traveled in a day. Amount of work done, amount of people seen/ interacted with, time until due date, among a few others. All of those seemed hard to visualize except the due date one, this one also resonated with me more. As a student a lot of my schedule is based around school and when I have to turn in projects. Due dates hae a hold on my life and cause some anxiety sometimes. Anxiety could be represented through a heartbeat or pulse, and this pulse could change based on how close the current date and time is to the given due date.

Reflection

This project was a fun way to experiment with booleans and logic. Setting a value based off of another value for the time was confusiong at first, and extracting the user inputs was a little hard to figure out. Once I had that figured out I just had to figure out the logic, which was tedious. All the different else if() statements had to not conflict with each other so the whole thing could work.
